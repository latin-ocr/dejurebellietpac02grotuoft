Ι. What Aristotle wrote is most true, that certainty is not to
be found in morals as it is in mathematics: of which the reason is
this, that mathematios separate the forms of things altogether from
matter: and that the forms are of such a nature that there is
nothing intermediate between two of them3 as between a straight
line and a curve there is no medium. But in moral matters, circum-
stances, even the least, often vary the matter3 and the forms that are
treated have often intermediate degrees, of such latitude that the case
sometimes approaches nearer to the one extreme, sometimes to the
other. Thus between that which ought to be done, and that which

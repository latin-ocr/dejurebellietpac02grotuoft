of the deceased, he is not bound to his punishment, as Paulus says the
commentators* have decided, this is the true cause3 that the heir
represents the person of the deceased, not in his deserts, which are
matters merely personal, but in his possessions3 and with them are
inseparably connected his debts 3 and accordingly this rule came in as
soon as ownership came in. So Dio Prusaeensis.

absolutely to be exacted, as in crimes of very bad example 3—or may
be fit not to be exactcd, as if the public good require it to be omit-
ted ;—or either course may be allowable: when, as Seneca says, Cle-

don, which they call spariny. In fact here and elsewhere, as Cicero,

Stoics is about words, which a philosopher ought carefully to avoid.
